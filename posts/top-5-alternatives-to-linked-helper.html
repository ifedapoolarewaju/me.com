Top 5 Alternatives to LinkedHelper - Thursday, 13 November 2025

LinkedHelper is a well-established LinkedIn automation tool, but its desktop-based nature and sometimes complex interface can lead users to look for alternatives. Whether you're seeking a more user-friendly experience, a cloud-based solution, or a more affordable option, there are many great choices available.

In this guide, we'll explore the top 5 alternatives to LinkedHelper, comparing their features, pricing, and unique strengths to help you find the perfect fit for your LinkedIn outreach strategy.

1. Bindago

  • Website: https://bindago.com/
  • What it does: A comprehensive desktop application for LinkedIn automation that covers bulk connection requests, mass InMail campaigns, multi-step message sequences, and CSV exports. Because it runs locally on your computer, your credentials are never shared with a third-party server, offering a similar security model to LinkedHelper but with a more modern and intuitive interface.
  • Pricing: $9.99/month (flat rate for all features)
  • Key Features:
    • Bulk connection requests with personalized notes
    • Multi-step messaging sequences
    • Mass InMail sending to Sales Navigator leads
    • Bulk withdrawal of pending invitations
    • Export LinkedIn search results to CSV
    • Runs locally for maximum security
    • 10-day free trial (no credit card required)
  • Platform: Desktop App (Windows/Mac/Linux)
  • Best For: Professionals and small businesses looking for a secure, affordable, and easy-to-use alternative to LinkedHelper.

Why Bindago is the #1 Alternative: Bindago offers the same security benefits of a desktop application as LinkedHelper but with a much simpler and more user-friendly interface. At just $9.99/month, it's also more affordable than LinkedHelper's Pro plan while offering a complete suite of automation features. It's the perfect choice for those who want the power of desktop automation without the steep learning curve.

2. Octopus CRM

  • Website: https://octopuscrm.io/
  • What it does: A popular Chrome extension that automates various LinkedIn tasks, including sending connection requests, messages, and viewing profiles. It's known for its simplicity and affordability.
  • Pricing:
    • Starter: $9.99/month
    • Pro: $14.99/month
    • Advanced: $21.99/month
    • Unlimited: $39.99/month
  • Best For: Users who want a simple, budget-friendly, browser-based tool for basic LinkedIn automation and prefer a Chrome extension over a desktop app.

3. Expandi.io

  • Website: https://expandi.io/
  • What it does: A powerful cloud-based automation tool that mimics human behavior with smart sequences, connection requests, and follow-ups. It also offers multi-channel capabilities (LinkedIn + Email).
  • Pricing: Starts at $99/month per user/seat.
  • Best For: Agencies and teams needing a cloud-based solution with advanced AI-powered personalization and multi-channel outreach, and who are willing to pay a premium for it.

4. Dux-Soup

  • Website: https://www.dux-soup.com/
  • What it does: One of the oldest and most well-known Chrome extensions for LinkedIn automation. It automates profile visits, sending connection requests, and messaging.
  • Pricing: Paid plans start at €12.99/month.
  • Best For: Individuals looking for a reliable and straightforward browser extension to automate top-of-funnel activities.

5. Waalaxy

  • Website: https://waalaxy.com/
  • What it does: A cloud-based tool with a user-friendly interface that focuses on creating multi-channel prospecting sequences (LinkedIn + Email). It has a visual campaign builder that makes it easy to design outreach flows.
  • Pricing: Starts around €56/month for paid plans, with a limited free plan available.
  • Best For: Marketers and sales professionals who want to combine LinkedIn and email outreach in a single platform with an intuitive visual builder.

Conclusion: Which LinkedHelper Alternative is Right for You?

Choosing the right tool depends on your priorities:

  • For the Best Value and Security: Bindago is the clear winner. It provides a comprehensive set of features and a modern UI for just $9.99/month, while maintaining the security of a desktop application.
  • For Simplicity and Low Cost: Octopus CRM and Dux-Soup are great entry-level options if you prefer a simple browser extension for basic automation.
  • For Advanced Cloud Features: Expandi.io is a powerful, albeit expensive, option for those who need cloud-based multi-channel outreach with AI capabilities.
  • For Visual Campaign Building: Waalaxy is a strong contender if you want to integrate email into your LinkedIn sequences and prefer a visual workflow builder.

While LinkedHelper is a robust tool, these alternatives offer a range of options to fit different needs and budgets. For most users seeking a balance of power, security, and ease of use, Bindago offers the perfect modern alternative.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are desktop apps safer than cloud-based tools?
A: Generally, yes. Desktop applications like Bindago and LinkedHelper run on your computer, so your LinkedIn login credentials are not stored on a third-party server, reducing the risk of data breaches.

Q: Can I get banned for using these tools?
A: All automation tools carry some risk as they are against LinkedIn's Terms of Service. However, tools that simulate human behavior with smart delays and have built-in safety limits (like Bindago) significantly reduce this risk. Always start with low volumes and use the tools responsibly.

Q: Do I need Sales Navigator to use these tools?
A: It's not strictly required, but highly recommended. Sales Navigator provides much better search filters and higher commercial use limits, which allows you to get the most out of any automation tool.
